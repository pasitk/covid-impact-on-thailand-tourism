COVID-19 has devastated Thailand's tourism,
and recovery is still uncertain.
The number of tourist arrivals around the world has dropped 56% per cent according to the World Tourism Organization (UNWTO), after international travel almost vanished because of the Coronavirus pandemic. This is part of the 2020 economic loss that is three times that of the global economic crisis that began in 2007. A country which has necessarily been affected by this calamity is Thailand, where the tourism industry is one of the most important sectors of the economy. Businesses and people in this industry are suffering.
"I was a photographer for a tourism guide company, and I used to travel in a lot of provinces in Thailand. However, when the COVID-19 outbreak began, I had no job for 3 months. My company may be out of business now I think." says Pornpon Thanasuwannatarn who lost his job during the pandemic period, and then became a food delivery driver and restaurant owner to earn a living.
He adds that the sign of danger appeared during Chinese New Year period, or around last week of January, "From that time, many tour guides started to talk or post on their Facebook profile, asked that what should they do if the lockdown period would come. Some of them started selling foods, returning to their hometowns, or change their careers permanently."
His income fell from at least $160 per week to only $32-64 per month. He tells us that his savings are running out, and he is going to sell goods at the market from August.
His story is just one of many in this difficult time. There are also other opinions from the people in the tourism industry in this article, which I interviewed them on the phone.
In the big picture, Thailand is popular among tourists around the world because of its beautiful nature, cultural attractions, and the famous Thai food. In 2019, the receipt from international and Thai visitors was around $96 billion, or 17.83% of Thailand's gross domestic product in 2019.

However, if we look just only receipts from international tourists nowadays, it is around 10% of GDP, so it has increased a lot."
"There are more than three million employees who work in tourism-related sectors, from 40 million workers. So, the tourism-related sectors are huge and very important to employment status." Kittipong adds.
Tourism receipts dropped dramatically, with difficult recovery
The COVID-19 pandemic has damaged all sectors of Thailand's economic system, including tourism. The warning signs started when China, the biggest source of international tourists for Thailand, suffered from an outbreak in the last months of 2019. This was followed by the announcement to ban Chinese people from booking overseas tours in January 2020. The number of foreign visitors fell from that month on and hit zero in April, with a ban on commercial flights and the borders closed as part of a state of emergency. Meanwhile, the number of Thai visitors fell dramatically as well.
This means the income from tourists vanished. Total receipts from all visitors in the first four months of 2020 fell to only half of what they were in the same period of 2019.
It seems like the tourism sectors in Thailand are now in a recovery period nowadays. There had been only a few new domestic infected people in these recent months, and lockdown measures were eased to a point at which everything is almost back to normal.
