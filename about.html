About this project and methodology
About the project
This is a dissertation project as a part of the course MSc Computational and Data Journalism at Cardiff University produced by me, Pasit Kongkunakornkul. It comprises five features about the impact of COVID-19 on Thailand's tourism industry in the period since the outbreak started until September 2020, with multiple visualisations to enhance readers' understanding. The first article focuses on the overall impact and the effect on Thailand's economy, and the other four articles focus on each interesting business related with tourism: Aviation (focuses on pilots), Food delivery, Tour operator, and Accommodation.

Additional Remarks
  • All of the money values in these features were calculated by using the exchange rate: $1 = 31.25 Thai Baht.
  • All of the data provided by Thailand's Ministry of Tourism and Sports can be revised by the ministry later.
  • Thailand's GDP has been compiled quarterly by Thailand's Office of the National Economics and Social Development Council (NESDC) based on chain volume measure (CVM). CVM measures the quantitative change in production or expenditures at constant prices compared to the previous year in the form of 'Direct Index'. Then connect the cumulative growth rate in each year from the initial year, called as 'Chain Index' to provide a continuous series of data. The GDP data can be revised by NESDC later.
Definitions
  • Visitor: The person who is a tourist or an excursionist.
  • Tourist: The person who travels from his/her permanent residence to another place, to stay overnight at least one night but not more than 90 days. He/She will do any activities but must not get any salary or pay from his/her destination(s).
  • Excursionist: The person who travels from his/her permanent residence to another place, without staying overnight. He/She will do any activities but must not get any salary or pay from his/her destination(s).
  • MICE: Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions
